日本語

When I was in high school, we were required to take a language course for at least three full years. Being a somewhat stereotypical Texan, I chose to take Spanish, and had great fun doing so. I even ended up doing a three week long language trip to Seville, Spain, with a few teachers from my school, and learned a lot during my time there. After that trip, I took my third (and final) year of Spanish at my school.

The teacher for that class was a remarkable man - brilliant at what he taught, and even more exceptional at the teaching itself. Later in that year, I found out he was actually half Japanese, and off-handedly asked him about resources for learning the language. I received a slew of helpful information from him, with personal anecdotes about how he learned Spanish and English, and what his approaches were to the nuances of language learning.

Since then, I've been fascinated with languages, namely Japanese. One of the most noteable differences Japanese has from English (besides the different writing system) is that Japanese has a different sentence structure from English. Combining this with other features, such as being phonetically consistent and having over 50,000 borrowed and adapted characters from Chinese, I was very quickly absorbed into the world of learning this language.

On this page, I hope you can learn a little bit more about the language I love, and browse the resources I have acquired in my time learning Japanese. Scroll down for something fun!
